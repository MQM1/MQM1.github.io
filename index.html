This data set shows many different aspects of energy usage and consumption by source: electricity in different categories, petroleum, natural gas, coal, and Annual Energy Outlook, among others.

DESCRIPTION:

This database produces data on the movement of goods in the United States. It provides information on commodities shipped, their value, weight, and mode of transportation, as well as the origin and destination of shipments of commodities.

WHY:

This data set could be very informative with respect to teaching users/viewers about American commerce with respect to commodities, especially considering location and movement to point of use/consumption. From an economics perspective, this data set could provide a good basis for exploratory data visualizations and create a learning tool for understanding commerce and economy.

SOURCE: data.gov

DESCRIPTION:

This data set shows information on the buying habits of American consumers, including data on their expenditures, income, and consumer unit (families and single consumers) characteristics.

WHY:

Spending behaviors and cost allocations would be insightful information to understand how Americans spending their money, what their priorities are, and possibly see if location has an effect on this. It's also interesting to see just how much information is available in this data set with respect to people's perception of their privacy.

SOURCE: data.gov

DESCRIPTION:

The core of the dataset is the feature analysis and metadata for one million songs, provided by The Echo Nest. The dataset does not include any audio, only the derived features.

WHY:

From an exploratory perspective, this dataset might provide some interesting insights on patterns or similarities in music and lyrics. Music is something that interests many people, and features like iTunes Genius tends to fascinate users with it's ability to find "similar songs" that sound nothing like the original. This is a potentially exciting prospect: exploratory data visualizations in music.

SOURCE: opensciencedatacloud.org

DESCRIPTION:

The 1000 Genomes Project is an international effort to create a detailed catalog human genetic variation.

WHY:

Genetics has become a hot topic for exploration in recent years in response to scientific advancement and reduction in cost of analysis. This could be a very rich data set in exploring Human Genetics in almost any aspect, such as trait-to-ethnicity mappings, regional differences, percent-of-population traits, and any trends therein.The Human Genome is not only interesting to scientists, but also the general public, as it information regarding genetics gives us insightful understanding of our history and evolution.
